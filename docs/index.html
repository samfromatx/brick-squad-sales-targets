💡 Price Ratio Framework
Raw → PSA 9: Should You Buy Raw and Grade?
Break-even formula: Raw price < (PSA 9 value × gem rate) − grading cost
With ~$38–45 all-in grading cost, use these rules of thumb:
ScenarioRule
Raw card is under 40% of PSA 9 value✅ Strong candidate to grade — if the card grades well
Raw card is 50–60% of PSA 9 value⚠️ Marginal — condition must be near-perfect
Raw card is 70%+ of PSA 9 value❌ Just buy the PSA 9
Example: PSA 9 sells for $100. Raw sells for $35 (35% ratio). Grading costs $42. If it grades a 9 → profit $23. If it grades an 8 (PSA 8 ≈ $55) → lose $22. You need realistic confidence it grades 9+.
PSA 9 vs PSA 10: The Multiplier Decision

Population data matters most here. The gem rate flips this logic — a 50% gem rate compresses the multiplier over time; a 10% gem rate means PSA 10s are genuinely scarce and the premium may hold.

PSA 10 / PSA 9 RatioDecision
Under 2×Buy PSA 9 — strong value
2×–3×PSA 9 still favored unless gem rate is very low
3×–5×Situational — depends on gem rate and player trajectory
5×+PSA 10 may be worth it if gem rate is under 20%
⚡ Quick Decision Cheat Sheet
Low gem rate (<20%) + PSA 10 sells 4× the PSA 9 → Buy / chase PSA 10
High gem rate (>40%) + PSA 10 sells 2× the PSA 9 → Buy PSA 9 all day
Raw card <40% of PSA 9 + clean card → Grade it
Raw card >60% of PSA 9 → Buy the slab

Why Football = PSA 9, Basketball = PSA 10: Football Prizm cards tend to have lower gem rates, so PSA 10s carry a bigger premium that's harder to justify at most price points. Basketball Prizm gems are more liquid and the PSA 10 market is deeper — PSA 10 is the market standard.

Watch: For rising star rookies, raw prices can compress fast — the window to grade profitably closes quickly as raw prices chase the PSA 9 comp.
